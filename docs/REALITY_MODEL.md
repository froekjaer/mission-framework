# Reality Model

> **Software does not operate on reality. It operates on representations of reality.**

Mission Framework begins with a distinction that is simple, obvious and frequently ignored:

Reality exists independently of systems.

Sensors do not create reality.

Humans do not create reality.

AI does not create reality.

They observe, record and interpret it.

## The reality gap

No model contains the full richness of reality. Every observation is selective, every recording is incomplete and every interpretation is provisional.

The distance between reality and its representation is the **reality gap**.

The objective is not to eliminate the gap. That is impossible.

The objective is to make the gap visible, bounded and continuously challenged.

## The reality lifecycle

```text
Reality
  ↓
Observable Reality
  ↓
Observed Reality
  ↓
Recorded Reality
  ↓
Validated Reality
  ↓
Shared Knowledge
  ↓
Human Understanding
  ↓
Decision
```

Each transition loses, transforms or adds information.

### Reality

What exists and occurs independently of the framework.

### Observable reality

The portion of reality that could be detected from the available position, time, instruments and human capabilities.

### Observed reality

What was actually perceived by a person, sensor or system.

### Recorded reality

What was preserved in a durable representation.

### Validated reality

Recorded observations that have passed defined integrity, provenance and quality checks.

### Shared knowledge

Structured claims and relationships constructed from validated evidence.

### Human understanding

The contextual interpretation used to support judgement and action.

## Irreducible uncertainty

Some uncertainty can be reduced through better sensors, stronger evidence, more time or improved analysis.

Other uncertainty is irreducible within the mission's practical limits.

Mission Framework distinguishes:

- **avoidable uncertainty** arising from insufficient effort, poor controls or missing information
- **irreducible uncertainty** that remains after reasonable observation and analysis

A trustworthy system does not hide either form.

## Observability

Unknown does not mean false.

Not observed does not mean absent.

An observation has meaning only in relation to the capability and conditions of the observer.

A failed detection can result from:

- absence of the phenomenon
- insufficient sensitivity
- wrong position or time
- obstruction or interference
- failed equipment
- unsuitable method
- missing expertise

The framework must preserve this distinction.

## The observer principle

Observation changes the mission record.

Once an observation is made, the observation itself becomes a historical event with an observer, method, time and context.

Reality has memory through consequence.

Systems have memory only when they deliberately preserve it.

## Multiple observations of reality

Different observers perceive different aspects of the same reality.

A camera, temperature sensor, operator, maintenance record and AI detector may all describe the same event differently without any one representation being complete.

Truth emerges through synthesis, challenge and continuing observation rather than through the authority of a single source.

Mission Framework therefore preserves:

- source perspective
- observation conditions
- time and location
- measurement capability
- known limitations
- contradictions
- unresolved interpretations

## Time is a first-class dimension

Reality changes.

A statement may be correct at one time and false at another. Evidence may be authentic yet stale. A dependency may exist during normal operation but disappear during disruption.

Time must therefore be represented in claims, observations, dependencies and trust assessments—not added later as incidental metadata.

The framework distinguishes, where relevant:

- event time
- observation time
- recording time
- ingestion time
- validation time
- decision time
- action time
- period of validity

## Reality anchoring

### Reality Anchor Principle

*Inspired by the Danish expression “Remtræk til virkeligheden” by Morten Thrane.*

> **Every model, inference, recommendation and decision shall maintain a continuously verifiable anchor to observable reality.**

Reality anchoring is stronger than merely citing a source. It requires the organisation to know how recently and how directly a claim has been tested against reality.

A reality anchor weakens when:

- evidence becomes stale
- provenance is broken
- transformations are opaque
- detection capability is unknown
- assumptions are no longer tested
- models operate outside their validated context
- repeated citations lead back to the same unverified source

A broken anchor does not automatically make a conclusion false. It makes the conclusion less justified.

## The principle of humility

Every model is provisional.

A mature system must be capable of saying:

- we do not know
- the evidence is incomplete
- the sources disagree
- the condition may have changed
- the model may be wrong
- further observation is required

This is not weakness. It is operational honesty.

## Engineering consequences

Mission Framework must never confuse:

- a database with reality
- an observation with the whole event
- evidence with truth
- a model with understanding
- confidence with correctness
- consensus with fact
- absence of detection with absence of condition
- a current state with a permanent property

## Closing statement

Mission Framework is an architecture for maintaining humanity's conversation with reality.

Its purpose is not to replace reality with better models.

Its purpose is to ensure that models, decisions and actions never lose their **remtræk til virkeligheden**.
