# Existence and Value Test

**Status:** Normative evaluation method  
**Version:** 1.0

## Core question

> Does Mission Framework enable a mission to create demonstrably better justified outcomes than a credible alternative, at a proportionate cost?

The framework's continued development is justified only while the evidence supports a useful answer to this question.

## What counts as value

Value may be demonstrated through one or more of the following, provided the effect is observable and relevant to the mission:

- improved mission outcome
- earlier detection of error, contradiction or missing evidence
- stronger decision traceability
- better calibrated trust and uncertainty
- successful independent reconstruction
- reduced rework or avoidable failure
- improved continuity across people, tools or time
- clearer accountability and delegation
- improved accessibility or stakeholder comprehension
- reduced operational, legal, safety or governance risk

A benefit is not sufficient if its cost or burden is disproportionate.

## Comparison requirement

Each existence test shall compare framework use with a credible alternative. The preferred comparison order is:

1. controlled or paired execution of the same bounded task
2. an established professional method applied to the same task
3. a comparable historical execution
4. structured expert assessment with disclosed limitations

The alternative must be described fairly. A deliberately weak comparison is invalid.

## Evaluation dimensions

Each test shall report at least:

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| Outcome | Was the mission objective achieved more effectively? |
| Justification | Are important claims and decisions better supported? |
| Reconstruction | Can an independent reviewer understand what happened and why? |
| Error detection | Were contradictions, omissions or regressions found earlier? |
| Effort | What additional time, skill and tooling were required? |
| Burden | Did governance or documentation obstruct useful work? |
| Transferability | Could another participant continue or reproduce the work? |
| Adverse effects | What new risks, delays or distortions were introduced? |

## Decision thresholds

A single test does not decide the existence of the entire programme. Evidence accumulates by proposition and release.

### Continue

Continue investment when evidence shows material value in at least one important dimension, no unacceptable adverse effect, and a credible path to reduce observed burden.

### Revise

Revise when the intended value appears plausible but the method is ambiguous, excessively burdensome, difficult to reproduce or dependent on undocumented expertise.

### Deprecate a concept

Deprecate a concept when repeated tests show negligible value, disproportionate cost, persistent confusion or performance inferior to a simpler alternative.

### Pause expansion

Pause conceptual or platform expansion when validation evidence is too weak to justify added complexity.

### Consider programme closure or absorption

Consider closure, archival status or absorption into established methods when, across multiple credible tests:

- no distinctive value is demonstrated
- claimed benefits are adequately achieved by simpler existing methods
- costs remain disproportionate after revision
- independent use cannot be achieved
- the programme depends on the original author to function

## Foundation v1.1 initial test

Mission Solar Eclipse shall be the first bounded existence test.

The initial proposition is:

> A small Mission Framework vertical slice enables an independent reviewer to reconstruct mission purpose, evidence, decisions, actions and outcomes more completely and with fewer unresolved assumptions than a comparable conventional project record.

The test shall capture:

- reconstruction completeness
- unresolved assumptions
- time required
- contradictions detected
- participant effort
- artefacts judged unnecessary
- changes made because of the framework

## Reporting rule

Existence-test results shall be published whether positive, negative or inconclusive.

The conclusion shall identify:

- what was demonstrated
- what was not demonstrated
- the tested context
- remaining uncertainty
- the decision taken
- the accountable owner of that decision

## Principle

Mission Framework is not validated by being internally consistent, comprehensive or admired.

It is validated only to the extent that reality shows it creates useful, proportionate and reproducible improvement.
