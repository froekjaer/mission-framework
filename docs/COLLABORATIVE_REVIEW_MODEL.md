# Collaborative Review Model

## Why this matters

Mission Framework has been shaped through collaboration between a human steward and multiple independent AI systems with different strengths and perspectives.

This is not treated as proof that the framework is correct.

It is treated as a method for exposing assumptions, disagreement and blind spots earlier.

## Current collaboration pattern

The early framework has involved:

- **Human stewardship** — purpose, judgement, lived context, accountability and final acceptance of consequential changes;
- **ChatGPT / OpenAI** — architectural synthesis, continuity, drafting and integration;
- **z.ai** — independent cross-cutting architectural and philosophical challenge;
- **Codex** — technical architecture, implementability, provenance, lifecycle and conformance challenge;
- **Claude** — semantic, conceptual and editorial challenge.

These roles describe contributions in this phase. They are not permanent identities or exclusive authorities.

## Principle of independence

Independent reviewers should, where practical, review the artefacts themselves before being shown other reviewers' conclusions.

This reduces convergence caused merely by imitation and makes genuine convergence more informative.

Agreement is evidence of shared interpretation, not proof of truth.

Disagreement is useful evidence about ambiguity, assumptions or competing design choices.

## Human accountability

AI systems may contribute analysis, critique, drafts and recommendations.

Human accountability is not transferred merely because multiple systems agree.

Consequential framework decisions remain subject to explicit human-governed acceptance and traceable change control.

## Review synthesis

Review findings should be preserved rather than flattened into a single anonymous answer.

Synthesis should distinguish:

- independent convergence;
- partial agreement;
- unique insight;
- unresolved disagreement;
- rejected recommendations and rationale.

This preserves the epistemic value of plurality.

## Avoiding artificial consensus

The objective is not to make all reviewers agree.

A healthy review process allows:

- different ontological assumptions;
- different risk tolerances;
- different implementation concerns;
- different interpretations;
- explicit minority positions.

Consensus that is produced by hiding disagreement is less useful than documented disagreement.

## Reality remains outside the panel

Neither a human nor an AI review panel is the final authority on whether the framework works.

Real missions produce observations and evidence that may contradict every reviewer.

Therefore:

```text
Independent Review
        ↓
Provisional Semantic Improvement
        ↓
Real Mission
        ↓
Observation and Evidence
        ↓
Framework Findings
        ↓
Revision
```

The collaboration is valuable precisely because it remains corrigible.

## A model for future participation

Future contributors may include:

- domain practitioners;
- researchers;
- architects;
- operators;
- affected stakeholders;
- additional AI systems;
- independent implementers.

No contributor should need to adopt the worldview of the original authors in order to challenge the framework.

The framework should become stronger by making criticism easier to express, trace and test.

## Long-term aspiration

Mission Framework should be capable of outliving the particular people and systems that created its first versions.

That requires:

- explicit semantics;
- preserved provenance;
- open disagreement;
- reproducible decisions;
- independent comprehension;
- reality-based revision.

The goal is not a framework created by one intelligence.

The goal is a framework that different forms of intelligence can understand, challenge, improve and use together — while remaining accountable to reality.
