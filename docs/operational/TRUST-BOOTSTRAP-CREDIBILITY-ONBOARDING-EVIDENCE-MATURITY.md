# Trust Bootstrap, Credibility Onboarding and Evidence Maturity

**Status:** Operational concept specification  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Owner:** Mission Framework governance

## Purpose

Mission Framework may appear more mature than its public age suggests because a small number of working days can formalise experience, evidence and design work accumulated over many years. This creates a predictable credibility risk: external readers may confuse rapid publication with rapid invention.

This document defines three operational mechanisms for handling that risk without exaggeration, concealment or borrowed authority.

## 1. Trust Bootstrap

Trust Bootstrap is the controlled establishment of initial confidence before independent validation exists.

It MUST rely on inspectable evidence rather than prestige claims. Appropriate mechanisms include:

- transparent repositories and publication history;
- explicit scope and maturity statements;
- traceable sources and historical evidence;
- known limitations and unresolved questions;
- reproducible generation and review processes;
- separation of fact, inference, assumption and judgement;
- invitation to falsification rather than requests for endorsement.

Trust Bootstrap does not establish that the framework is correct. It establishes that the work is sufficiently transparent and bounded to deserve examination.

## 2. Credibility Onboarding

Credibility Onboarding is the process by which a new human stakeholder is helped to understand the project, its provenance, its present maturity and the most efficient path through its evidence.

The first contact MUST be an introduction, not an assurance assignment.

The standard awareness package is:

1. a concise introductory message;
2. a generated Project Extract presentation;
3. the generated Mission Framework book;
4. repository and provenance references.

The introduction MAY mention MIAR as an optional later pathway. It MUST NOT imply that the recipient, their organisation or the associated AI system has endorsed, accredited or validated the project.

Credibility Onboarding SHOULD explain accurately that:

- the present public framework was assembled over a short, disclosed period;
- the underlying professional experience, prior research and source material pre-date that publication period;
- AI accelerated structuring, drafting, comparison and publication;
- human governance remains responsible for claims, decisions and release approval;
- independent assurance is still pending or incomplete where applicable.

## 3. Evidence Maturity

Evidence Maturity is the assessed strength and operational relevance of evidence supporting a claim, design decision or capability.

Evidence maturity is separate from confidence and separate from verification status. A claim can be verified as accurately quoted from a design document while still having low maturity as evidence of real-world fitness.

### Initial maturity scale

| Level | Name | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| EM0 | Hypothesis | Idea, question or proposed relationship without supporting implementation evidence. |
| EM1 | Documented design | Defined rationale, model or specification with traceable authorship. |
| EM2 | Implemented prototype | A working implementation or demonstrator exists. |
| EM3 | Internally reproduced | The result has been reproduced by the project team under documented conditions. |
| EM4 | Independently reproduced | A materially independent party has reproduced or validated the result. |
| EM5 | Externally reviewed | Evidence has survived a relevant formal external review, audit or peer-review process. |
| EM6 | Operationally sustained | The capability has performed over time in real operational use with monitored outcomes. |

The scale is ordinal, not automatic. Movement between levels requires recorded evidence and may be reversed when contradictions, defects or context changes emerge.

## Relationship between the mechanisms

```text
Trust Bootstrap
      ↓
Credibility Onboarding
      ↓
Evidence Maturity growth
      ↓
Independent Assurance (for example MIAR)
      ↓
Operational adoption and continued evidence
```

The sequence is not a sales funnel. It is an evidence and governance lifecycle.

## Operational rules

1. Public age, development duration and evidence age MUST be distinguished.
2. Rapid AI-assisted production MUST be disclosed when material to interpretation.
3. Historical evidence MUST NOT be presented as proof of current framework validity.
4. Generated books and presentations MUST identify their canonical sources, generation date and applicable repository commits.
5. The recipient of an introductory package is not being assigned work.
6. MIAR or another review process may be offered only as a voluntary next step.
7. No external organisation may be represented as participating, endorsing or reviewing until that status is verified.
8. Evidence-maturity labels MUST be supported by a traceable evidence record.

## Review questions

Independent reviewers should test whether these mechanisms:

- clarify provenance without creating a misleading origin narrative;
- reduce credibility friction without becoming marketing language;
- distinguish accumulated experience from current project evidence;
- prevent generated artefacts from overstating maturity;
- provide a usable and falsifiable evidence-maturity model;
- create appropriate boundaries between introduction, dialogue and assurance.
