**Introduction**

This report will focus on building and implement a product and service innovation framework focusing on the Nordic regional business unit of ADP Dealer Services International sub-division.

The US based company, ADP has two major business areas separated into two primary business unites:

- ES: Employer Services, that delivers Payroll services and HR Management as a service to its clients.

- DS: Dealer Services, that delivers Dealer Managements Systems to Automotive Dealers.

The ES and DS divisions are handling the US national business, where the international sub-divisions ESI and DSI are taking care of the International business.

ADP Dealer Services International HQ resides in Hungerford, UK, where the Nordic HQ resides in Vantaa, FI.

The overall purpose of this executive report is to build a framework[^1] to prescribe which actions ADP need to take to be able to provide the needed products and services to its clients in a near and long-term future.

The study will describe how to analyze the process of product and services selection, where client needs are identified and discussed.

Included in the study, will be analyses of future and current states to build a strategy based on identified gaps to facilitate the journey.

Parts of the discussion will be looking at HR processes to ensure the required personal capabilities are available to the business at a given time. This also includes requirements to the business to identify their needs.

# Purpose of the report 

The overall purpose of this report is to present and implement a framework to suggest which actions ADP-DSI-Nordic should initiate to fulfill the Automotive Dealer marked need for products and services by 2017 and 2025. Therefore, the goal of the report is via analysis and comparison of the current state and the desired strategy, to suggest how to handle identified gaps.

The overall purpose of this executive report is:

To build and implement a framework to recommend which actions ADP need to take to be able to provide the needed services to its clients in a near and long-term future

ADP has identified an immediate and future need to maintain and expand its market share in the Nordic region.

There is a real need to build a process to identify marked need, and address the identified needs in a way that will benefit the clients of ADP by expanding and developing their business where at the same time benefitting the business of ADP.<span class="mark">

To ensure that the needed competences and capabilities are available within the organization – the discussion around insourcing vs. outsourcing are very important. # Finance[^6]

## Implementation cost and timeline

To implement and maintain the Innovation Process, one FTE is needed as the key-stakeholder.

Additionally at least one weekly – one hour meeting with an average of 5 associates is expected.

The baseline cost of maintaining the program is \$200K/y

The expected implementation time within the whole Nordic organization is expected to be one calendar year.

## Funding

Financially, implementing and maintaining the Innovation Process could be dealt with as an infrastructure investment similar to a CRM or ERP system, and could be treated the same.

## Per project cost and timeline

Based on numbers from another ODI project and educated estimates, the expected timeline of a simple project is expected to be:

Kickoff and project development 4 weeks

Qualitative study 4 weeks

Quantitative study 3 weeks

Analyses and segmentation 3 weeks

Preparation for R&D 4 weeks

Total 18 weeks

There is expected an average of 5 associates fulltime involvement, resulting in an approximate cost of \$80k per project.

## Project-specific financial considerations

In the project initiation phase, one important task is to consider the expected financial outcome of the process.

The cost of the process must not exceed the expected outcome.

If a required financial growth is being addressed by the focal process, this must be taken into considerations while planning and executing the process. The Segmentation part is where the big wins are expected to be found.

# Reflection

Implementation of the Innovation Process based primarily on Outcome Driven Innovation will allow for ADP-DSI-Nordic to gain the role of Innovation Leader

The Innovation Process of ADP-Nordic must be uniformed to align with the Division and thereby corporate strategy.

At the same time, there must though, be implemented a local manifestation adjusting the ADP-Nordic strategic operationalization to Nordic and in-country marked, culture and local needs.

HR must take a more active role in maintaining and aligning associates skills with company needs in sense of Core Competences and Business & Technical skills.

Implementing the Innovation Process in the Nordic organization is challenging, and the Internal Sell is probably going to be the most delicate part of the implementation process.

Gaining financial endorsement in an organization where innovation doesn’t even have its own place in the budget - could also pose an interesting challenge.

Looking at Outcome Driven Innovation: One of the most crucial tasks and at the same time, the least specific addressed issue is Segmentation.

The described Innovation Process is trying to address the need – in the best possible manner.
