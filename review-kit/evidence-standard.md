# Evidence Standard

Version: 1.0  
Status: Approved  
Category: Core Review Standard

---

## Purpose

This standard defines what constitutes acceptable evidence within the Mission Framework.

The objective is to ensure that conclusions, recommendations, assessments, and review decisions are based on verifiable evidence rather than unsupported opinion.

---

## Fundamental Principle

Claims SHALL be supported by evidence.

The burden of proof lies with the person or function making the claim.

Absence of evidence SHALL NOT be interpreted as evidence of absence.

---

## Evidence Quality

Evidence SHALL be evaluated using the following attributes.

### Authenticity

Evidence SHALL originate from an identifiable and trustworthy source.

The source and method of acquisition SHOULD be documented whenever practical.

### Integrity

Evidence SHALL be protected from undocumented alteration.

Any transformation, redaction, conversion, or summarisation SHALL be traceable.

### Relevance

Evidence SHALL directly support the claim, finding, or conclusion for which it is cited.

Unrelated information SHALL NOT be used to create an artificial impression of support.

### Timeliness

Evidence SHALL reflect the timeframe relevant to the review.

Outdated evidence SHALL be identified and its limitations explained.

### Sufficiency

Evidence SHALL be sufficient to support the strength of the conclusion.

Independent corroboration SHOULD be obtained whenever practical, especially for material findings.

### Reliability

The reliability of the source, collection method, and evidence type SHALL be considered.

Known limitations, uncertainty, and possible bias SHALL be documented.

---

## Evidence Categories

Acceptable evidence MAY include:

- legislation and regulatory material
- recognised standards and official guidance
- contracts and formal agreements
- official documentation and records
- policies, procedures, and approved designs
- configuration data and system inventories
- audit logs and event records
- source code and version history
- photographs, recordings, and measurements
- test results and system outputs
- interviews and written statements
- direct observations

The category alone does not determine quality. Each item SHALL still be evaluated against the attributes in this standard.

---

## Source Hierarchy

Sources SHOULD be prioritised in the following general order, subject to context:

1. Primary evidence and original records
2. Binding legal or contractual sources
3. Official records and authoritative documentation
4. Independently verified expert analysis
5. Reputable secondary publications
6. Unverified statements or personal opinion

A lower-ranked source MAY be used when stronger evidence is unavailable, but the limitation SHALL be stated.

---

## Evidence Identification

Material evidence SHOULD receive a stable identifier.

An evidence record SHOULD include:

- evidence identifier
- title or description
- source
- acquisition date
- relevant timeframe
- owner or custodian
- integrity information, where applicable
- confidentiality classification
- related findings or conclusions
- known limitations

---

## Conflicting Evidence

Conflicting evidence SHALL NOT be ignored.

The reviewer SHALL:

- document the conflict
- assess the relative quality and reliability of the sources
- explain remaining uncertainty
- identify assumptions
- avoid conclusions that exceed the available support

Where the conflict prevents a justified decision, the outcome SHOULD be `Unable to Conclude`.

---

## Interviews and Statements

Interviews and personal statements MAY be used as evidence, but SHOULD be corroborated for material conclusions.

Records of interviews SHOULD include:

- participant
- interviewer
- date
- scope
- key statements
- confirmation or review status

Opinions SHALL be distinguished from observed facts.

---

## AI-Generated Material

AI-generated content SHALL NOT be treated as evidence by itself.

AI MAY be used to:

- summarise evidence
- classify or organise evidence
- identify inconsistencies
- support search and discovery
- suggest additional evidence

AI output SHALL be validated against the underlying source material before it supports a finding or decision.

Material AI-assisted analysis SHOULD record the model or service used, the relevant inputs, and the validation performed.

---

## Traceability

Every material finding and conclusion SHALL reference the evidence that supports it.

Evidence identifiers SHOULD remain stable throughout the review lifecycle.

Where a conclusion relies on inference, the inference SHALL be clearly distinguished from the underlying evidence.

---

## Handling and Protection

Evidence SHALL be handled according to its sensitivity and applicable requirements.

Controls MAY include:

- access restrictions
- encryption
- integrity hashes
- chain-of-custody records
- secure transfer
- redaction
- controlled disposal

Redaction SHALL preserve sufficient context for the evidence to remain understandable and verifiable.

---

## Retention and Disposal

Evidence SHALL be retained according to:

- legal and regulatory requirements
- contractual obligations
- organisational policy
- review and appeal needs

Retention periods SHOULD be documented.

Disposal SHALL be authorised, appropriate to the sensitivity of the evidence, and recorded when required.

---

## Prohibited Practices

Reviewers SHALL NOT:

- fabricate or alter evidence
- omit material conflicting evidence
- present assumptions as facts
- cite a source without reviewing the relevant content
- rely on AI-generated claims without source validation
- overstate the certainty or scope of the evidence
