# Terminology Standard

Version: 1.0
Status: Approved
Category: Core Review Standard

---

## Purpose

This standard defines how terminology shall be selected, defined, used, and maintained across Mission Framework reviews and supporting material.

The objective is to ensure that language remains consistent, understandable, precise, and suitable for both specialist and non-specialist stakeholders.

---

## Fundamental Principle

Terms SHALL be used consistently throughout a review.

The same concept SHALL NOT be described using different terms unless the distinction is intentional and documented.

---

## Defined Terms

Terms that are central to the scope, method, findings, decisions, or recommendations of a review SHALL be defined.

Definitions SHOULD be:

- clear
- concise
- unambiguous
- appropriate to the intended audience
- aligned with authoritative sources where relevant

---

## Normative Language

The following words have specific meanings when used in Mission Framework standards:

- SHALL indicates a mandatory requirement.
- SHALL NOT indicates a prohibition.
- SHOULD indicates a recommended practice.
- SHOULD NOT indicates a practice that is normally discouraged.
- MAY indicates an optional practice.

Alternative wording SHALL NOT be used to weaken or obscure a mandatory requirement.

---

## Acronyms and Abbreviations

An acronym or abbreviation SHALL be written in full at first use unless it is universally understood by the intended audience.

Acronyms and abbreviations SHOULD be included in a glossary when they are used repeatedly or may be ambiguous.

---

## External Terminology

Where terminology originates from legislation, regulation, standards, contractual material, technical specifications, or recognised professional practice, the source SHOULD be identified.

Externally defined terms SHALL NOT be redefined in a way that changes their meaning without explicit explanation.

---

## Ambiguous Terms

Ambiguous, subjective, or context-dependent terms SHOULD be avoided.

Where such terms are necessary, the intended meaning SHALL be documented.

Examples include:

- adequate
- significant
- material
- reasonable
- timely
- high risk

---

## Controlled Vocabulary

Projects MAY establish a controlled vocabulary for recurring concepts.

A controlled vocabulary SHOULD identify:

- preferred term
- definition
- permitted synonyms
- prohibited or deprecated terms
- source or authority

---

## Terminology Conflicts

Where stakeholders use conflicting definitions, the review SHALL:

- identify the conflict
- select or establish the working definition
- document the rationale
- apply the selected definition consistently

A terminology conflict that may affect findings or decisions SHALL be treated as a review issue.

---

## Translation

Translated terms SHALL preserve the intended meaning of the source language.

Where no exact equivalent exists, the original term SHOULD be retained together with an explanatory translation.

Machine translation MAY assist the process but SHALL be reviewed when terminology affects evidence, findings, risk, decisions, or recommendations.

---

## AI Support

AI MAY assist with:

- detecting inconsistent terminology
- identifying undefined acronyms
- proposing glossary entries
- comparing definitions
- highlighting ambiguous language

AI SHALL NOT invent authoritative definitions or sources.

---

## Maintenance

Terminology definitions SHOULD be reviewed when:

- scope changes
- new evidence is introduced
- external requirements change
- ambiguity is identified
- a review is revised or repeated

Changes to significant definitions SHALL be traceable.

---

## Review Requirement

Every completed review SHALL use consistent terminology and SHALL define terms whose interpretation could materially affect the review outcome.

---
