# Review Decision Standard

Version: 1.0  
Status: Approved  
Category: Review Kit Standard

---

## Purpose

This standard defines the possible outcomes of a Mission Framework review.

The objective is to ensure that review conclusions are:

- consistent
- repeatable
- evidence-based
- understandable by all stakeholders

Review decisions SHALL NOT be based on personal opinion alone.

---

## Decision Principles

A review decision SHALL:

- be supported by documented evidence
- reference applicable standards where possible
- distinguish observations from facts
- explain residual uncertainty
- remain proportional to identified risk

---

## Decision Types

### APPROVED

**Meaning:** The reviewed material satisfies all mandatory requirements.

Typical characteristics:

- no Critical findings
- no High findings
- Medium findings are absent or formally accepted
- required documentation is complete

**Recommended action:** Proceed.

### APPROVED WITH CONDITIONS

**Meaning:** The reviewed material is acceptable provided specified conditions are completed.

Typical characteristics:

- limited Medium findings
- Low findings
- agreed corrective actions with owners and deadlines

All conditions SHALL be documented and traceable.

### CHANGES REQUIRED

**Meaning:** The review identified deficiencies that prevent approval.

Typical characteristics:

- one or more High findings
- repeated or systemic Medium findings
- incomplete evidence
- missing mandatory documentation

The reviewed artefact SHALL be corrected and resubmitted.

### REJECTED

**Meaning:** The artefact cannot reasonably proceed in its current state.

Typical reasons:

- Critical findings
- unacceptable risk
- major governance failure
- absence of required evidence
- violation of mandatory standards

A complete redesign or substantial rework MAY be necessary.

### UNABLE TO CONCLUDE

**Meaning:** The reviewer cannot reach a justified conclusion.

Reasons MAY include:

- insufficient evidence
- unavailable stakeholders
- conflicting evidence
- scope limitations
- missing documentation

This is not a failed review. It indicates that additional evidence or clarification is required.

---

## Decision Matrix

| Highest finding | Typical decision |
|---|---|
| None | Approved |
| Low | Approved |
| Medium | Approved with Conditions |
| High | Changes Required |
| Critical | Rejected |

Professional judgement MAY justify another decision. Any deviation from the matrix SHALL be documented with rationale and evidence.

---

## Required Documentation

Every review decision SHALL include:

- decision type
- decision summary
- evidence references
- reviewer identity
- review date
- unresolved issues
- assumptions and limitations
- required or recommended actions
- action owners and target dates, where applicable

---

## Independence

Review decisions SHALL remain independent from:

- project pressure
- management preference
- financial considerations
- delivery deadlines

Evidence and applicable requirements SHALL take precedence.

---

## Reconsideration and Closure

A decision MAY be reconsidered when:

- new material evidence becomes available
- conditions have been completed
- the reviewed artefact has materially changed
- an error in the original review is identified

Changes to a decision SHALL be recorded with the reason, date, reviewer, and evidence references.
