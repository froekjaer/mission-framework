# Project Principles

Mission Framework is guided by principles rather than technologies.

These principles should outlive every programming language, framework, cloud platform and AI model used within the project.

## 1. Trust by Design

Trust is the primary architectural principle.

Every significant component, workflow and decision should be evaluated by whether it increases or decreases justified trust.

## 2. Evidence over Assumptions

Claims should be supported by evidence whenever possible. Assumptions must be identified, recorded and open to challenge.

## 3. Provenance Matters

Important knowledge must have a traceable origin. Sources, transformations and material AI contributions should be visible.

## 4. Humans Remain Accountable

Technology may assist, recommend and automate. Humans remain responsible for consequential decisions.

## 5. Unknown is Better than Wrong

When evidence or confidence is insufficient, Mission Framework should communicate uncertainty rather than manufacture certainty.

## 6. Architecture before Technology

Technologies evolve. Architectural intent and constraints should endure.

## 7. Independence by Design

Mission Framework should avoid unnecessary dependence on specific vendors, AI providers, cloud platforms or implementation technologies.

## 8. Simplicity Scales

Systems that can be understood are easier to verify, operate, secure and maintain.

## 9. Security, Privacy, Safety and Resilience Support Trust

Trust by Design does not replace these disciplines. It unifies their contribution to systems that deserve confidence.

## 10. Everything Evolves

Architecture is expected to improve as missions create new evidence and learning.

## 11. Learn Continuously

Every mission should contribute reusable knowledge back to the framework without compromising confidentiality, ownership or mission independence.
