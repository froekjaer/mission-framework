# Mission Framework

Mission Framework is an emerging semantic foundation for describing, governing and improving missions across domains. It places mission outcomes, operational reality, evidence and traceable decision-making ahead of organisational convenience.

## Why it exists

Complex initiatives often fail because intent, authority, evidence, operational conditions and claimed progress become disconnected. Mission Framework proposes a shared vocabulary and a disciplined mission loop for keeping those elements aligned.

## Core ideas

- **Mission before structure:** organisational arrangements serve the mission rather than define it.
- **Reality Principle:** claims and plans must be revised when operational evidence contradicts them.
- **Evidence as the currency of trust:** confidence should follow traceable evidence, not presentation quality.
- **Semantic foundation:** concepts should be understandable across people, organisations and machines.
- **Independent challenge:** review and falsification are built into development rather than postponed until completion.

## Current maturity

The framework is in pre-review development. Its public formalisation has been rapid, while the experience, research and historical material informing it extend further back. Historical material demonstrates provenance and continuity; it does not by itself validate the current framework.

## Trust and publication

The project uses Trust Bootstrap, Credibility Onboarding and Evidence Maturity to make uncertainty visible. Publications are generated from canonical Markdown sources with source revision, provenance and hashes. Generated PDFs, presentations and web pages remain derived artefacts rather than independent sources of truth.
