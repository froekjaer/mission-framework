# Decision Principles

Mission Framework is guided by purpose before process.

These principles are not rigid rules. They are a compass for moments of uncertainty, disagreement, and change.

## Choose purpose over popularity

The best decision is not always the most popular.

Decisions should support the purpose of Mission Framework and the people it is intended to help.

## Choose evidence over opinion

Opinions can begin a conversation.

Evidence should improve and guide it.

Where evidence is incomplete, assumptions should be made visible and tested.

## Choose simplicity over unnecessary complexity

Simple solutions are easier to understand, teach, maintain, test, and improve.

Simplicity must not remove essential nuance, responsibility, or safety.

## Choose stewardship over ownership

Knowledge should be shared.

Responsibility belongs to those willing to care for it.

No person, discipline, organisation, or technology owns the purpose of Mission Framework.

## Choose collaboration over isolation

Meaningful problems rarely fit within one discipline.

We seek perspectives that challenge blind spots and strengthen understanding.

## Choose understanding over false certainty

Questions can create more progress than premature answers.

We distinguish between what is known, what is assumed, and what remains uncertain.

## Choose people over technology

Technology enables.

People create purpose, accept responsibility, and experience the consequences.

Technology should serve people, not reverse that relationship.

## Choose accessibility over exclusivity

Knowledge has limited value when those who need it cannot understand, obtain, or use it.

We explain clearly and remove unnecessary barriers wherever possible.

## Choose continuity over dependence

Mission Framework must not depend on one individual.

Knowledge, responsibility, and stewardship should be distributed over time.

## Choose the future over short-term convenience

Decisions should help the next contributor understand what was done and why.

Important reasoning should be documented, and history should be preserved rather than rewritten.

## When in doubt

Return to the purpose:

> **Create what is needed, and make it accessible to those who need it.**

Then leave the work better than you found it.
