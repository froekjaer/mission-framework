# Invitation to an Independent Reviewer

Dear reviewer,

I would like to invite you to review an early open initiative called **Mission Framework**.

The project explores a simple but demanding question:

> How can genuine needs become trustworthy and sustainable value through missions that remain grounded in reality?

This is not a request for endorsement. I would value a candid, thoughtful and independent assessment—especially where the project is unclear, overambitious or built on assumptions that have not been examined closely enough.

## The three repositories

Please review these public GitHub repositories in order:

1. `froekjaer/mission-framework`
2. `froekjaer/Mission-Platform`
3. `froekjaer/mission-solar-eclipse`

They serve different purposes:

- **mission-framework** contains the principles, theory and shared vocabulary.
- **Mission-Platform** explores how the ideas may become practical architecture and tools.
- **mission-solar-eclipse** is the first test against a real planning and execution challenge.

You do not need to be a software developer to contribute. The review is equally relevant from the perspectives of architecture, governance, operations, research, leadership, safety, communication, philosophy or practical mission planning.

## What I would especially value

Please look for:

- ideas that are difficult to understand without additional explanation
- definitions that appear circular, overlapping or too abstract
- assumptions that may not hold outside the examples used
- gaps between principles and practical operation
- concepts that seem unnecessary or important concepts that are missing
- tensions between human responsibility and AI-assisted work
- weaknesses in the treatment of evidence, uncertainty and trust
- inconsistencies between the three repositories
- places where the framework may become bureaucratic rather than useful

## Three central propositions

The project currently rests on several propositions that should be challenged rather than accepted automatically.

### Reality before models

Models are tools. When credible evidence contradicts a model, the model must be reconsidered.

### A deliberately small Mission Core

A concept should belong in Mission Core only when it is indispensable across domains and cannot be handled adequately by a domain-specific extension.

### Human accountability

AI may assist, analyse and recommend, but consequential accountability remains human and organisational.

Please comment freely if these statements are incomplete, misleading or impractical.

## How to respond

A response template is available in this directory, but you are welcome to answer in your own format.

Please distinguish, where practical, between:

- what you directly observe in the material
- how you interpret it
- what you recommend changing
- what remains an open question

Brief comments are valuable. A complete review is not required. It is better to identify one important weakness precisely than to provide broad but polite approval.

You may return your comments directly to me, add them to the response document, open a GitHub issue, or propose a pull request. No changes will be treated as canonical without review and an explicit decision.
