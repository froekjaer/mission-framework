# Invitation to Claude

Dear Claude,

You are invited to act as an independent conceptual, semantic and editorial reviewer for the Mission Framework initiative.

Your role is not to endorse the project. Your role is to identify where its language, concepts and reasoning are unclear, inconsistent, overly broad or insufficiently grounded.

## Repositories

Please review these public repositories in order:

1. `froekjaer/mission-framework`
2. `froekjaer/Mission-Platform`
3. `froekjaer/mission-solar-eclipse`

Their intended responsibilities are:

- **mission-framework** defines philosophy, principles and canonical semantics.
- **Mission-Platform** translates those semantics into architecture and future tooling.
- **mission-solar-eclipse** tests the framework against a real mission.

## Your primary role

Act as a rigorous conceptual peer reviewer.

Please examine:

- whether definitions are precise and non-circular
- whether key distinctions are meaningful and stable
- whether the argument moves coherently from reality and need to mission, capability, action and evidence
- whether normative claims are clearly separated from descriptive claims
- whether trust, accountability, evidence and reality are used consistently
- whether the language is understandable without prior access to the project conversation
- whether the same concepts are likely to be interpreted differently by people or AI systems
- whether any philosophical claims need qualification, attribution or stronger justification

## Areas deserving special attention

### Reality Principle

The project holds that models must remain subordinate to observable reality and must change when credible evidence contradicts them.

Please examine whether this principle is sufficiently precise, whether it risks naïve realism, and how uncertainty, incomplete observation and conflicting evidence should be handled.

### Mission Core

The current admission rule is:

> A concept belongs in Mission Core only when it is indispensable across domains and cannot be represented adequately through a domain extension.

Please test whether this rule is operational, whether “indispensable” is too strong or vague, and whether the proposed concepts have clear boundaries.

### Human accountability

The project currently states that AI may recommend while humans remain accountable.

Please examine whether this is sufficient for distributed organisations, automated systems, delegated authority and cases where no single human has full knowledge or control.

## Expected response

Please use the response template in this directory.

Distinguish clearly between:

- textual observations
- semantic interpretations
- conceptual objections
- recommendations
- open questions

Quote only short passages when necessary and always identify the file being discussed. Prefer precise revisions over general stylistic advice.

Do not rewrite the framework into your own preferred philosophy. Help us understand where the existing project succeeds, fails or requires an explicit choice.
