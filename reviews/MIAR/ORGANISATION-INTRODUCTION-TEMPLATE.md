# Mission Framework — organisation introduction template

**Status:** Human-facing introduction template  
**Purpose:** First contact only; not a review assignment

## Required preparation by the focal AI agent

Before completing this introduction, the focal AI agent shall:

1. finish and freeze its independent MIAR Phase 1 conclusions;
2. identify a suitable real person or official organisational function using verified public information;
3. record the recipient's name, title, organisation and public professional contact route;
4. adapt the tone and ordering to the organisation's culture without changing the factual boundaries below;
5. verify the generated Project Extract and book against their canonical sources and record generation date and commit references;
6. return the completed introduction for human approval; it shall not send it autonomously.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

The introduction must not:

- assign the recipient a review task;
- imply endorsement, accreditation, partnership or organisational participation;
- describe the framework as mature, proven or independently validated unless supported by current evidence;
- conceal that the coherent public framework was formalised over a short period;
- imply that historical source material proves current validity;
- describe an AI model's output as the official position of its provider.

## Draft introduction

**Subject:** An introduction to Mission Framework — open work on evidence, governance and human–AI collaboration

Dear **[verified recipient name or organisational function]**,

I am writing to share an early open-source project that may be relevant to your work in **[verified area of relevance]**.

Mission Framework is an emerging framework for describing, governing and improving missions involving people, organisations, technology and AI systems. Its current public form has been assembled rapidly through AI-assisted work, but it draws on earlier professional practice, research and source material. We are therefore deliberately distinguishing the age of the public project from the age and maturity of its underlying evidence.

The project is still being tested. We are not seeking endorsement, accreditation or a commitment to perform a review through this first contact. The purpose is simply to offer a clear introduction and make the material available should it be of interest.

The accompanying awareness package contains:

1. **Mission Framework Project Extract** — a concise presentation generated from the canonical project sources;
2. **Mission Framework Book** — a longer generated reference edition;
3. **Repository reference** — the public source, history, evidence and review material needed to inspect the work directly.

The project is developing explicit operational concepts for:

- **Trust Bootstrap** — establishing initial confidence through transparency and bounded claims;
- **Credibility Onboarding** — helping a new stakeholder understand provenance, maturity and evidence efficiently;
- **Evidence Maturity** — distinguishing documented ideas, prototypes, reproduction, external review and sustained operation.

An independent assurance programme, MIAR, also exists, but it is intentionally a separate and optional next step. No response beyond acknowledgement is expected.

Should the work be relevant to you or a colleague, we would value an informal indication of which aspects appear worth further examination and which claims or assumptions should be challenged first.
