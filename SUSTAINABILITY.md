# Sustainability

Mission Framework is designed to continue beyond its founders.

Knowledge should not depend on a single person.

Responsibility should be shared.

Leadership should evolve.

Stewardship should be transferable.

The project must respect the real capacity of the people who care for it. Contribution is voluntary, and no contributor should be expected to sustain the framework through personal overextension.

## Human sustainability

People have changing health, work, family, and life circumstances.

Participation may grow, pause, or end.

That is normal.

Mission Framework should make room for:

- different levels of capacity
- temporary pauses and sabbaticals
- shared responsibility
- asynchronous contribution
- clear boundaries
- graceful transfer of stewardship

A sustainable project does not depend on exhaustion.

## Knowledge sustainability

Continuity requires that:

- important knowledge is documented
- processes are understandable
- decisions are explained
- assumptions are visible
- history is preserved
- work can be resumed by others

No essential knowledge should remain only in one person's memory.

## Stewardship sustainability

Stewards care for the work; they do not own it.

The framework should develop new stewards over time and distribute responsibilities according to interest, competence, trust, and available capacity.

A steward may become active, take a sabbatical, complete a period of service, return later, or become emeritus.

Their contribution remains part of the project's history.

## Organisational sustainability

Mission Framework should grow deliberately.

We prefer:

- quality before quantity
- understanding before expansion
- purpose before popularity
- resilient structures before rapid growth

Growth is valuable only when it strengthens the purpose.

## Technical sustainability

Tools, platforms, and technologies will change.

The purpose and knowledge of Mission Framework should remain portable, understandable, and recoverable.

Where practical, the project should favour:

- open formats
- version history
- clear repository structures
- minimal unnecessary dependencies
- documented maintenance procedures
- accessible backups and succession paths

## Financial sustainability

Mission Framework should avoid creating unnecessary financial barriers or obligations.

Funding, sponsorship, or commercial participation may support the work, but must not compromise the purpose, openness, governance, or independence of the Living Body of Knowledge.

## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence can accelerate research, writing, analysis, review, and implementation.

It cannot replace human stewardship or accountability.

AI supports.

People decide.

Important AI-assisted contributions should remain understandable and reviewable by people.

## Independence

Mission Framework succeeds when it continues to help people regardless of who currently maintains it.

It must never depend entirely on one founder, steward, organisation, sponsor, technology, or AI system.

## A sustainable pace

There is no requirement to finish everything quickly.

Pausing is not failure.

Rest is not abandonment.

The work can continue when capacity returns or when others are ready to carry it forward.
