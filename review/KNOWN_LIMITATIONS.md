# Current Limitations

Mission Framework is a working hypothesis and an evolving body of knowledge. It should not be treated as an empirically validated universal theory.

Current limitations include:

- limited empirical validation across domains
- a small number of practical reference cases
- evolving terminology and boundaries
- incomplete mapping to relevant academic literature
- unknown generalisability across cultures, organisations and mission types
- limited long-term operational evidence
- possible overlap with established disciplines and methods
- provisional governance and review mechanisms
- dependence on the quality, provenance and accessibility of observations
- unresolved questions about measuring better understanding and better decisions

These limitations are documented to guide criticism and research. They are not intended as disclaimers that protect the framework from challenge.

Statements in Mission Framework should be distinguishable as observations, evidence-supported claims, working definitions, hypotheses, normative principles or implementation guidance. Where that distinction is unclear, reviewers are asked to flag it.

The framework should ultimately be judged through criticism, practical application, reproducible experience and evidence of outcomes.
