# AI and AI-Assisted Review Track

Mission Framework welcomes structured review performed with contemporary artificial intelligence systems.

AI-generated review is analytical input. It is neither authority by declaration nor a substitute for accountable human judgement.

## Recommended process

1. Record the model, provider, date and relevant configuration.
2. State which documents were provided to the system.
3. Ask the system to distinguish factual errors, unsupported claims, conceptual weaknesses, inconsistencies and practical risks.
4. Request counterexamples and alternative terminology.
5. Preserve disagreements between models rather than averaging them away.
6. Require a human contributor to review consequential findings before disposition.

## Suggested prompts

- Identify claims that appear stronger than the available evidence.
- Find circular, overlapping or inconsistent definitions.
- Compare the framework with established disciplines and standards.
- Propose falsification tests for the central propositions.
- Identify incentives, governance failures and misuse cases.
- Identify what should be removed rather than expanded.

## Reporting

The review should identify the AI system used and clearly separate model output from human interpretation.

Different systems are expected to disagree. Such disagreement is useful evidence about ambiguity, assumptions and model dependence.
