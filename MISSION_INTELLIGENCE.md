# Mission Intelligence

Mission Intelligence describes how observations become trusted support for decisions and learning.

## Lifecycle

```text
Observation
    ↓
Information
    ↓
Knowledge
    ↓
Understanding
    ↓
Intelligence
    ↓
Decision
    ↓
Action
    ↓
Learning
```

The lifecycle is not always linear. New evidence may challenge earlier knowledge, and learning may require a previous decision to be revisited.

## Observation

An observation is a recorded event, measurement, statement or artefact.

Examples include:

- a satellite image
- an AIS position
- a weather report
- a government publication
- a sensor reading
- a human observation

Observations should retain time, source, context and provenance whenever possible.

## Information

Observations become information when context and meaning are added.

Information should distinguish recorded facts from interpretation and assumptions.

## Knowledge

Knowledge emerges when information is connected, tested and placed within a useful model of the mission.

Knowledge remains revisable when evidence changes.

## Understanding

Understanding explains relevant relationships, causes, constraints and uncertainty.

It addresses questions such as:

- What happened?
- Why might it have happened?
- What changed?
- What remains unknown?
- What could invalidate this interpretation?

## Intelligence

Intelligence is knowledge and understanding shaped for a defined decision or action.

It should communicate:

- relevant evidence
- provenance
- confidence and uncertainty
- assumptions and limitations
- plausible alternatives
- recommended actions and trade-offs

Mission Framework deliberately separates intelligence from decision authority.

## Decision

A decision selects a course of action within a defined mandate and risk context.

Mission Framework supports decisions. It does not remove human accountability for consequential choices.

## Action

Actions should remain traceable to their decision, rationale, evidence and accountable owner.

## Learning

Every completed action may create new observations and evidence.

Learning should improve the current mission and, where appropriate, contribute reusable knowledge to the wider framework.

## Trust throughout the Lifecycle

Trust is not a final quality check. It must be designed into every transition:

- observation to information
- information to knowledge
- knowledge to intelligence
- intelligence to decision
- decision to action
- action to learning

This is **Trust by Design** applied to mission intelligence.
