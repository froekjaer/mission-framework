# Trust by Design

> Trust is not a feature. It is the foundation.

## Purpose

Mission Framework exists to support systems that deserve trust.

Not blind trust. Not assumed trust. Justified trust.

Every architectural decision should strengthen the ability for people to understand, verify and responsibly act upon information.

## Working Definition

Within Mission Framework, trust is:

> Justified confidence that information, processes, recommendations and actions are supported by sufficient evidence, appropriate transparency and accountable reasoning.

Trust is not binary. It is contextual, dynamic and revisable.

## Trust Dimensions

### Source Trust

- Who produced the information?
- Is the source authentic and relevant?
- Can the source be verified?

### Evidence Trust

- What evidence supports the claim?
- Is the evidence sufficient and current?
- Can it be independently validated?

### Provenance Trust

- Where did the information originate?
- How was it transformed?
- Which people, systems and AI models influenced it?

### Process Trust

- Is the method documented?
- Can another qualified person reproduce or challenge the conclusion?
- Were assumptions, limitations and conflicts recorded?

### Decision Trust

- Can the recommendation be explained?
- Is the accountable human decision-maker identifiable?
- Is the decision proportionate to the evidence and uncertainty?

### Operational Trust

- Does the system continue to deserve trust over time?
- Are drift, degradation and changed conditions detected?
- Can trust be reduced or withdrawn when evidence changes?

## Trust States

Mission Framework may use the following working states where appropriate:

0. **Unknown** — insufficient evidence to assess
1. **Observed** — recorded but not independently verified
2. **Verified** — authenticity or accuracy checked within a defined scope
3. **Corroborated** — supported by multiple sufficiently independent sources or methods
4. **Trusted** — justified confidence for the defined purpose and context
5. **Highly Trusted** — strong, sustained and independently reviewable confidence for the defined purpose

These states are not universal scores. Every assessment must retain its context, criteria, evidence and date.

## AI and Trust

AI does not create trust merely by producing an answer.

AI output is a contribution that must be assessed according to its evidence, provenance, limitations and intended use.

Material AI involvement should be visible. Consequential decisions remain accountable to humans.

## The Architectural Test

Every significant feature, workflow and architectural decision should answer:

> **Does this increase or decrease justified trust?**

When a component does not strengthen trust directly, its purpose and trade-offs should still be explicit.

## Final Principle

Trust is earned.

Trust is maintained.

Trust can be lost.

Mission Framework is designed accordingly.
